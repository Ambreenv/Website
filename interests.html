INTERESTS

Travel

Whenever I can, I love to to travel and experience new cities and cultures. I have been lucky enough to visit many different cities around the world, allowing me to realize how each place is so unique no matter how similar it may seem to another city. Growing up, my parents always told me that travelling was the best education anyone could have, and now that I am older I definitely agree with them. Travelling to new countries has given me the opportunity to learn about the different cultures, traditions, and histories that exist in each city- all of which are things I wouldn't have been able to experience or appreciate in a classroom.

Music

Music and playing the piano have become an important part of who I am because it is the only thing I actually stuck with as a child throughout all the different phases that I went through (did someone say actor? singer? basketball player?). It has given me the ability to share my love of music with others by volunteering weekly to play the piano at retirement homes and weddings. I have developed a strong appreciation for the universal language of music and how it has the power to connect people and act as an escape.

Whenever I have time, I love playing the piano, freshening up my skills, and finding new songs that I could learn. I began playing the piano at the age of 5 and continued taking lessons, and practicing habitually, until I graduated highschool at 18. Throughout university it became harder to play the piano since I didn't have easy access to one, but whenever I found myself getting stressed I turned to it as a means of relaxation. Growing up I hated practicing the piano, but I am so glad I stuck with it because it has made me learn the importance of perserverance and hard-work, which are skills that I carry with me today.

Food

I love to try new and old types of food (and dessert) whenever and wherever I can, because they're never made the same in any one place. Trying a food I've eaten many times is always exciting when it's in a new restaurant or city because it allows me to experience foods I eat at home in a new way. Tasting foods from different cultures allows me to be more adventurous, learn more about the country, and expand my horizons! I've realized that some of the best Thai food that I've tried was in India (and of course they also had great Indian food), Waterloo has amazing Baos (better than most of the places that I've tried) and no one can beat the pasta in Italy (duh). Not only does trying new things make me more knowledgeable about different countries and cultures, but it is a big part of socialising and brings people together.
